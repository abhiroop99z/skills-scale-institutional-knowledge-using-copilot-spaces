# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers define user journeys, interaction patterns, and interface expectations so teams can deliver usable, accessible experiences that match product goals.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and interaction flows
- Document design decisions, accessibility requirements, and UX constraints
- Partner with Product Managers to validate user problems and solution direction
- Support Developers and QA with implementation-ready design guidance

### Goals
- Improve usability, accessibility, and user satisfaction
- Reduce rework caused by unclear or late design decisions
- Ensure delivered experiences align with user needs and product intent

### Lifecycle Fit
- **Initiation/Planning:** Contributes research insights and early flows.
- **Execution:** Supports design clarifications and UX acceptance.
- **Release/Retro:** Reviews user feedback and usability outcomes.

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and walkthroughs with Product and Engineering
- Prototype feedback sessions with stakeholders/users
- Clarifications in planning meetings, tickets, and PR comments

### Interactions / Hand-offs
- **Project Manager:** Aligns design milestones to project timeline and flags design risks/dependencies.
- **Product Manager:** Co-defines problem framing, prioritizes UX scope, and confirms acceptance criteria reflect UX intent.
- **Developers:** Provides implementation-ready designs and clarifies behavior during build.
- **QA/Testing:** Shares expected UX behaviors and accessibility checks for test coverage.
- **Stakeholders:** Presents prototypes and incorporates feedback before development lock-in.

## Scrum Masters

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate delivery flow by running Agile ceremonies, improving team processes, and removing impediments that block progress.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Coach the team on Agile practices and continuous improvement
- Identify and escalate blockers, dependencies, and process bottlenecks
- Partner with Project Managers on delivery predictability and risk visibility

### Goals
- Keep work flowing with minimal blockers
- Improve team health, collaboration, and iteration outcomes
- Increase reliability of sprint commitments

### Lifecycle Fit
- **Planning:** Facilitates planning and readiness discussions.
- **Execution:** Runs ceremonies, tracks blockers, and drives team flow.
- **Release/Retro:** Coordinates demos/retros and process improvements.

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation and blocker follow-ups
- Sprint planning/review/retro facilitation
- Regular syncs with PM, PdM, and delivery team leads

### Interactions / Hand-offs
- **Project Manager:** Shares sprint risks/escalations and aligns ceremony outputs with project reporting.
- **Product Manager:** Supports backlog readiness and helps maintain clear sprint goals.
- **Developers:** Facilitates execution rhythm, dependency resolution, and focused delivery.
- **QA/Testing:** Coordinates test-readiness discussions in sprint planning and review checkpoints.
- **Stakeholders:** Facilitates demo/review cadence to gather timely feedback.

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business needs into clear, testable requirements and maintain traceability from goals to delivered outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document functional/non-functional requirements
- Refine acceptance criteria with Product Managers and Developers
- Maintain requirement traceability across backlog, testing, and release artifacts
- Support UAT preparation and stakeholder validation activities

### Goals
- Reduce ambiguity in requirements and scope interpretation
- Improve decision quality with clear business context
- Ensure delivered capabilities map to intended outcomes

### Lifecycle Fit
- **Initiation/Planning:** Captures requirements and acceptance criteria.
- **Execution:** Clarifies scope and supports decision traceability.
- **Release/Retro:** Supports UAT readiness and outcome validation.

### Typical Communication
- Requirement workshops and clarification sessions
- Backlog refinement and acceptance criteria reviews
- Traceability updates in planning and release documents

### Interactions / Hand-offs
- **Project Manager:** Aligns requirement milestones with delivery timeline and change-control decisions.
- **Product Manager:** Co-authors requirements, acceptance criteria, and priority trade-offs.
- **Developers:** Clarifies business rules and edge cases before and during implementation.
- **QA/Testing:** Provides requirement traceability and expected behaviors for test case design.
- **Stakeholders:** Validates requirement intent and acceptance expectations before sign-off.

---

## Stakeholder Reviewers

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Reviewers represent business/customer interests at key milestones and provide formal feedback or sign-off on deliverables.

### Responsibilities
- Review milestone outputs for business fit and readiness
- Provide timely feedback on scope, priorities, and usability
- Confirm whether acceptance criteria and outcomes meet stakeholder expectations
- Participate in release and post-release review checkpoints

### Goals
- Keep delivery aligned with business/user expectations
- Surface risks early through structured review points
- Improve adoption confidence before release

### Lifecycle Fit
- **Initiation/Planning:** Validates problem framing and scope expectations.
- **Execution:** Provides milestone feedback in demos/reviews.
- **Release/Retro:** Participates in sign-off and post-release feedback.

### Typical Communication
- Milestone review meetings and async feedback threads
- Approval/sign-off comments on major deliverables
- Release-readiness and post-release feedback sessions

### Interactions / Hand-offs
- **Project Manager:** Confirms review timelines, decisions, and escalation paths.
- **Product Manager:** Reviews value alignment, priorities, and feature completeness.
- **Developers:** Provides feedback on delivered behavior and edge-case expectations.
- **QA/Testing:** Confirms UAT/business validation outcomes before release decisions.
- **Stakeholders:** Consolidates broader stakeholder feedback into clear go/no-go inputs.
